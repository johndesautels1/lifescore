# SECTION 2: LIFE SCORE OVERVIEW

## 2.1 What is LIFE SCORE?

**LIFE SCORE** stands for **Legal Independence & Freedom Evaluation**. It is a comprehensive system that compares legal freedom metrics between metropolitan areas to help people make informed decisions about where to live.

**The Core Question LIFE SCORE Answers:**
*"Which city gives me more legal freedom and autonomy to live my life as I choose?"*

**Key Features:**
- Compares 200 metropolitan areas (100 North America + 100 Europe)
- Evaluates 100 distinct freedom metrics across 6 life categories
- Uses 5 independent AI evaluators with web search for evidence
- Final consensus built by Claude Opus 4.5 ("The Judge")
- Generates beautiful 30-page visual reports via Gamma AI
- Provides you (Olivia) for personalized consultation

**What LIFE SCORE Measures:**
- Laws on the books (Legal Score)
- How laws are actually enforced in practice (Enforcement Score)
- Evidence from authoritative sources
- Trends toward more or less freedom

**What LIFE SCORE Does NOT Measure:**
- Cost of living (separate consideration)
- Weather/climate preferences
- Job market strength
- Cultural fit or social scene
- Language barriers
- Healthcare quality (beyond legal access)

## 2.2 The LIFE SCORE Philosophy

LIFE SCORE operates on these principles:

1. **Freedom is Measurable:** Legal restrictions and protections can be objectively compared
2. **Laws vs. Reality:** Written law differs from enforcement; we measure both
3. **Consensus Over Opinion:** Multiple AI perspectives reduce bias
4. **Evidence-Based:** Every score backed by verifiable sources
5. **Transparency:** Users can see exactly how scores were calculated
6. **Personal Relevance:** Not everyone values the same freedoms equally

**The Freedom Spectrum:**
- LIFE SCORE doesn't judge whether more or less regulation is "good"
- It measures the degree of government/private control over daily life
- Higher scores = fewer restrictions on personal autonomy
- Users decide what matters most to their lifestyle

# SECTION 4: SCORING METHODOLOGY

## 4.1 The Dual-Score System

Every metric receives TWO scores (0-100):

**Legal Score:** What does the written law technically say?
- 90-100: Fully legal/unrestricted
- 70-89: Generally permissive with minor limitations
- 50-69: Moderate restrictions
- 30-49: Significant restrictions
- 0-29: Prohibited/Illegal

**Enforcement Score:** How is the law actually enforced?
- 90-100: Never/rarely enforced
- 70-89: Low enforcement priority
- 50-69: Selectively enforced
- 30-49: Usually enforced
- 0-29: Strictly enforced (zero tolerance)

**Why Both Matter:**
- A city might have strict laws but rarely enforce them
- A city might have permissive laws but aggressive enforcement
- The combination reveals the true "lived freedom"

## 4.2 Scoring Direction

**Higher is Better (88 metrics):**
- More freedom = higher score
- Example: More abortion access = higher score

**Lower is Better (12 metrics):**
- Less burden = higher score (inverted)
- Examples: HOA prevalence, property tax, incarceration rate, police density, corporate tax, income tax, sales tax, transfer tax

## 4.3 Category Weighting Formula

**Weighted Average Calculation:**
```
Category Score = (Sum of MetricScore × MetricWeight) / (Sum of MetricWeights)
```

**City Total Score:**
```
Total = Sum of (CategoryScore × CategoryWeight) for all 6 categories
```

**Final Score Scale:** 0-100 (normalized)

## 4.4 Winner Determination

**Tie Thresholds:**
- **Overall:** Difference < 1 point = TIE
- **Category:** Difference < 5 points = TIE
- **Metric:** Difference < 2 points = TIE

**Winner Declaration:**
- Clear winner if difference exceeds threshold
- Ties declared when cities are functionally equivalent

## 4.5 Handling Missing Data

When data is unavailable:
- Score defaults to 50 (neutral)
- Marked as "unverified"
- Philosophy: Neutral (50) = "could go either way," not 0 = "bad"

**3. Gemini 3 Pro (Google)**
- Specialization: Google Search grounding + advanced reasoning
- Strength: Native Google Search integration
- Approach: Extra reasoning time on contradictions between legal text and enforcement

**4. Grok 4 (xAI/X)**
- Specialization: Real-time social enforcement data
- Strength: Uses X/Twitter to find resident experiences
- Approach: Bridges "legal theory" vs. "enforcement reality" gap using social posts

**5. Perplexity Sonar Pro**
- Specialization: Citation-backed research
- Strength: Built-in web search with numbered citations
- Approach: Authoritative, verified sources with full attribution

## 5.2 The Judge: Claude Opus 4.5

After the 5 evaluators complete their assessments:

1. **Statistical Consensus:** Calculate median and standard deviation for each metric
2. **Disagreement Detection:** Flag metrics where standard deviation > 15
3. **Opus Review:** Claude Opus 4.5 reviews high-disagreement metrics
4. **Final Judgment:** Opus provides reasoned overrides or accepts consensus

**Confidence Levels (based on standard deviation):**
- **Unanimous (σ < 5):** All LLMs nearly identical
- **Strong (σ 5-12):** Minor variation, good agreement
- **Moderate (σ 12-20):** Some disagreement visible
- **Split (σ > 20):** Major disagreement, sent to Opus

## 5.3 Overall Agreement Score

```
Overall Agreement = 100 - (Average Standard Deviation × 2)
```

- 100% = Perfect agreement (σ = 0)
- 90% = Very strong agreement (σ = 5)
- 75% = Good agreement (σ = 12.5)
- 50% = Moderate agreement (σ = 25)
