## Overview

This document is Vercel's Data Processing Addendum governing how Vercel processes personal data on behalf of customers. This DPA forms part of the Vercel Customer Terms of Service.

## Data Roles

| Data Type | Vercel's Role |
|-----------|---------------|
| Customer Data | Processor |
| Service-Generated Data | Controller |
| Contact Data | Controller |

## Data Processing

- Vercel processes customer data "to provide the Services"
- Processing follows documented customer instructions
- Data retained per termination provisions

## Security Measures

Technical safeguards include:
- AES-256 encryption at rest
- TLS 1.2+ encryption in transit
- Regular backups across AWS/Azure/GCP
- SOC 2 Type 2 audits
- Mandatory employee training

## Subprocessors

- Customers authorize use of Vercel's Affiliates and other vendors as subprocessors
- Current list maintained at security.vercel.com
- Customers can object to new subprocessors within 5 days based on "reasonable data protection concerns"
- Termination is exclusive remedy if unresolved

## Customer Responsibilities

- Obtain all necessary consents
- Ensure lawful data collection before processing through Vercel

## International Transfers

Vercel uses Standard Contractual Clauses and UK IDTA for lawful cross-border transfers from:
- EEA
- UK
- Switzerland
- Other jurisdictions

## Jurisdiction-Specific Terms

Includes provisions for:
- California (CCPA)
- EEA (GDPR)
- Switzerland
- UK
- Australia
- Canada

## Data Subject Rights

- Vercel assists with access, deletion, and portability requests
- Customers remain responsible for data subject inquiries
- Assistance may be at customer's expense for significant resource requirements

## Data Retention

- Customers can delete data on-demand
- Upon agreement termination, Vercel deletes all Customer Data within a "commercially reasonable timeframe"
- Exception: legally required retention
